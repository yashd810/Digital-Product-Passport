Service 03

How we help product companies become compliance ready

Product companies often feel the pain of this work in many teams at once: product, sustainability, procurement, quality, regulatory, and operations. We help reduce that burden with a structured pathway.

Compliance-ready DPP architecture

We help shape the passport architecture and data model so each required field has a realistic owner, evidence source, update route, and validation logic.

Validation against regulation requirements

We translate sector obligations into structured data expectations, then test whether the company can actually populate those fields at the needed quality level.

Environmental study guidance

We support the demanding studies that often feed the passport, including life cycle assessment and product carbon footprint calculations where regulation or market pressure requires them.

Chemical and product evidence mapping

We help organise REACH, RoHS, waste-directive, and CE-related evidence, especially for products like batteries where many supporting records sit behind the final passport.

Service 04

Help in digitising sustainability goals and building a stronger circular economy

Sustainability goals often stay stuck in reports, while operational teams still work with disconnected product evidence. We help bring those goals into a digital operating model so they can influence design, sourcing, service, and recovery.

From ambition to operational data

Many companies have sustainability targets for recycled content, carbon reduction, repairability, and circularity, but the product data needed to steer those targets is scattered. We help convert broad goals into governed product-level records that teams can actually use.

Once the data is structured, it becomes easier to compare product lines, spot weak supplier inputs, prioritise better material choices, and understand whether circular-economy ambitions are translating into measurable outcomes.

Making circularity more than a slogan

DPP programmes can support repair loops, reuse programmes, component recovery, recycled-content tracking, and better end-of-life routing. The passport becomes the digital thread that keeps those value loops visible.

That matters commercially as well as environmentally. Better visibility into lifecycle and recovery can support cost savings, stronger sustainability narratives, and smarter product-development decisions over time.
