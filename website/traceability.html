Service 03 · Regulatory Requirement

Carbon Footprint Made Simple

Product-level carbon calculation is one of the most technically demanding elements of DPP compliance — and a growing mandatory requirement. We strip away the complexity and deliver a regulation-ready study for every product in your portfolio.

Why Carbon Calculation Is So Complex

Most companies underestimate what product carbon footprint (PCF) calculation actually involves. It is not simply measuring what comes out of your factory chimney. Scope 3 emissions — the emissions embedded in your supply chain, your suppliers' suppliers, and the end-of-life disposal of your product — account for over 70% of a typical manufactured product's total carbon footprint, yet require data from hundreds of supplier tiers across multiple countries and processes.

⚠️ Regulatory mandate: EU Battery Regulation requires carbon footprint declarations from February 2025 for large stationary batteries, with performance class thresholds tightening from 2027. ESPR DPPs for electronics, textiles, and construction materials will require carbon intensity data from 2027–2028.

How ClarosDPP Simplifies It — 5 Steps

1

Product Data Intake & BOM Mapping

We collect your product's Bill of Materials, manufacturing energy data, and logistics information through a structured onboarding process — accepting data from ERP systems, spreadsheets, or our supplier portal. No special software or data format required from you.

2

Supplier Emission Factor Assignment

Our database maps each material and process to certified emission factors from ecoinvent 3, GaBi, and IPCC databases. Where primary supplier data is unavailable, we apply conservative, transparent secondary data with documented assumptions that hold up to auditor scrutiny.

3

Full ISO 14067 / PEF Lifecycle Assessment

We run a complete product carbon footprint calculation aligned to ISO 14067 and EU Product Environmental Footprint (PEF) methodology — covering all lifecycle stages from raw material extraction through manufacturing, distribution, use phase, and end-of-life disposal or recycling.

4

DPP-Ready Carbon Declaration

Results are structured and formatted for direct embedding into your Digital Product Passport — satisfying EU Battery Regulation Article 7 carbon intensity declarations and ESPR DPP data schema requirements. Third-party review available for enhanced credibility.

5

Reduction Roadmap & Scenario Modelling

We identify carbon hotspots across your product's lifecycle and model alternative sourcing, manufacturing process, and logistics scenarios — giving you an actionable roadmap to reduce your carbon intensity and stay ahead of tightening regulatory thresholds over time.
