Every Key Deadline

These are enacted regulations with enforceable deadlines — not proposals. The organisations that treat them urgently today will be the ones with functioning compliance systems by 2027.

2022
ESPR Foundation

ESPR Regulation Proposed by European Commission

The European Commission introduces the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR) as the legislative successor to the Ecodesign Directive. For the first time, Digital Product Passports are proposed as a mandatory instrument for making product sustainability information accessible across the entire value chain — not just for energy efficiency, but for full lifecycle transparency.

✓ Completed
2023
Batteries

EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542 Enters Force

The EU Battery Regulation — the most detailed sustainability product regulation ever enacted by the EU — enters into force. It introduces the first mandatory Digital Product Passport for any product category, with a phased implementation schedule. The regulation covers portable batteries, industrial batteries, EV traction batteries, and SLI batteries (automotive starting). Supply chain due diligence obligations and recycled content targets create cascading data requirements across the battery value chain globally.

✓ In Force
Feb 2025
Batteries

Battery Carbon Footprint Declarations — Enforceable

From February 18, 2025, carbon footprint declarations become mandatory for large-format stationary energy storage systems and industrial batteries above 2 kWh placed on the EU market. Companies must declare the product carbon footprint (kg CO₂e per kWh of energy capacity) following the EU-specified PEF-based methodology. This is the first regulatory enforcement of product-level carbon data in EU history — a landmark for mandatory PCF reporting globally.

✓ Active Now
2024–25 ← NOW
ESPR Published Textiles — Design Phase

ESPR Published · Textile DPP Delegated Act Consultations Begin

Regulation (EU) 2024/1781 (ESPR) is officially published in the EU Official Journal, establishing the binding DPP framework for all product categories. The first ESPR Working Plan identifies priority product groups for 2025–2027. Simultaneously, the European Commission launches stakeholder consultations on the Textiles DPP delegated act — widely expected to mandate passports for apparel and footwear from 2026. This is the window to start data collection. Companies that begin now will be test-ready by 2026.

● Active — Window to Act
2026
Textiles & Fashion

Textile & Fashion Digital Product Passport Mandatory

Passports for apparel, footwear, and textiles are expected to become mandatory across the EU per ESPR Working Plan. Fashion and sportswear brands selling in the EU must carry QR-accessible DPPs covering fibre composition, country of manufacture, durability ratings, wash-care instructions, recyclability data, and full supply chain transparency back to Tier 2 suppliers. Given the complexity of global fashion supply chains, companies that have not started data collection in 2024–2025 are at severe risk of non-compliance.

⚡ Approaching — Start Now
Feb 2027
Batteries Electronics

Full Battery DPP Mandatory · Electronics Phase-In

February 18, 2027 is the single most important compliance deadline for battery and EV manufacturers worldwide. All industrial and EV traction batteries placed on the EU market must carry a fully compliant Digital Product Passport, accessible via QR code and containing: carbon footprint declaration, supply chain due diligence data, recycled content percentages (cobalt, lithium, nickel, lead), SoH and SoC data schema for EV batteries, and safety information. Simultaneously, ESPR delegated acts for electronics and ICT equipment are expected to mandate DPP phase-in — starting the digital product passport era for consumer electronics in the EU.

⚡ Critical Deadline — 18 Months to Prepare
2027–28
Electronics Furniture Construction

Electronics, Furniture, Construction Materials — DPP Rollout

ESPR Working Plan second wave: consumer electronics, ICT equipment, furniture, construction products, steel, aluminium, and cement sectors face DPP phase-in. Carbon footprint requirements extend across all regulated categories. REACH integration becomes mandatory — SVHCs above 0.1% must appear in DPP data fields, making chemical substance tracking a core DPP infrastructure requirement for all industrial manufacturers.

○ Upcoming — Planning Phase
2028–29
Chemicals Tyres Agriculture

Chemicals, Tyres, Agricultural Products — Extended Scope

Third wave of ESPR delegated acts expands DPP requirements to the chemicals sector (linking DPPs with the upcoming SCIP database migration), tyre durability and microplastic data, and agricultural machinery. The EU's commitment to full supply chain transparency accelerates as circular economy objectives become embedded across every major industrial sector.

○ Future Wave
2030+
Universal Coverage

Universal DPP Coverage — The New Normal for EU Market Access

ESPR's long-term objective is near-universal coverage of products sold in the EU market. By 2030, Digital Product Passports will be as standard a requirement as CE marking is today — a permanent, expected component of market access for any product entering the European Union. Companies that built their DPP infrastructure early will have years of operational maturity, supplier relationships, and regulatory credibility over late movers.

○ Long-Term Vision
⏳

Why You Must Start Today — Not Next Year

Data aggregation across multi-tier supply chains takes 18–36 months on average. Supplier onboarding alone — collecting the chemical, origin, and energy data needed for a credible DPP — requires months of engagement with suppliers who may themselves need education and system setup. LCA and carbon footprint studies take 3–6 months per product category. System integration with your ERP, PLM, and supplier portal adds further lead time.

Companies that begin in 2026 are already operating with compressed timelines and higher risk. Companies that started in 2024 are ahead of the curve, accumulating supplier data, refining their compliance systems, and building competitive intelligence. Every quarter of delay is a quarter of disadvantage that cannot be recovered.
