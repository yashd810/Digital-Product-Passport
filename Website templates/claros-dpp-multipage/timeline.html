Regulatory clock

A clear timeline for DPP readiness.

The EU Digital Product Passport under ESPR is being introduced in phased waves. Some dates are fixed, while others depend on delegated acts, implementing measures, or pending legislative finalisation.

Conservative timeline design

Entries marked expected or phased should be treated as planning signals, not confirmed legal deadlines. Early preparation matters because data collection often takes longer than policy teams expect.

Direct mandatesSupporting layersHard deadlines
Ongoing baseline

REACH, RoHS, WEEE, Waste Framework Directive and SCIP

These obligations predate DPPs but define material, substance, and waste data that sector passports must expose.

2024Direct framework

ESPR enters into force — 18 July 2024

Establishes the legal framework for DPPs, the EU Registry, and the delegated act process for many product groups through 2030.

2025Framework action

ESPR Working Plan 2025–2030 adopted

Names textiles, furniture, tyres, iron and steel, aluminium, and electronics as priority groups for delegated act development.

2026Infrastructure

EU DPP Registry expected to go live

Central infrastructure and registry routes become part of the passport operating model. Teams should prepare identifiers and access logic early.

2027Hard deadline

Battery passport becomes mandatory — 18 February 2027

Every relevant LMT battery, industrial battery above 2 kWh, and EV battery placed on the EU market must carry a battery passport.

2027–2028Expected DPP tracks

Textiles, electronics, ICT, aluminium, tyres and construction routes mature

Delegated acts, sector-specific rules, and adjacent compliance layers begin translating into concrete passport fields.

2028–2030Rolling expansion

ESPR second wave and 30+ additional product groups

Further sectors follow Commission prioritisation, each embedding specific passport content requirements and ecodesign obligations.
