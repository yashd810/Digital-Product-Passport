1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Claros Digital Product Passport ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal data when you visit or use our platform, public Digital Product Passport viewer, APIs, dashboards, integrations, documentation, QR-code landing pages, support channels, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

The Service is a business-to-business SaaS platform for creating, managing, publishing, verifying, and accessing Digital Product Passports ("DPPs"). A DPP may include product, battery, facility, economic operator, compliance, lifecycle, circularity, sustainability, repairability, recycling, safety, technical, certification, and related documentation data.

Some DPP information may be intentionally made public through public passport pages, QR codes, public product identifiers, decentralized identifier documents, JSON-LD exports, verifiable credentials, public API endpoints, signatures, dictionary routes, or other standards-facing interfaces.

2. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • Website visitors who browse our website, documentation, marketing pages, or public DPP pages
  • Customer administrators and users who create accounts, log in, manage workspaces, use dashboards, publish DPPs, use APIs, or configure integrations
  • Invitees who receive invitations to join a customer workspace or company account
  • Public DPP viewers who access public passport pages, QR-code links, public APIs, DID documents, JSON-LD files, signatures, or verifiable credentials
  • Customer personnel, supplier contacts, facility contacts, and other individuals whose personal data may be included in customer-submitted content
  • Prospects, partners, vendors, and support contacts who interact with us in a business context

Cookie Policy

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, pixels, SDKs, and server-side tracking technologies. These technologies help websites work, remember preferences, maintain sessions, understand usage, and secure accounts.

2. How We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

We may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep you signed in
  • Maintain secure sessions
  • Remember cookie-consent choices

  • Measure performance and diagnose errors
  • Understand how visitors use the website and Service
  • Support customer support and troubleshooting
  • Provide analytics or marketing features, only where permitted and consented to if required

These cookies are required for the website and Service to function. They may include authentication cookies, session cookies, CSRF protection cookies, consent-management cookies, and security cookies. These cookies generally do not require consent under EU cookie rules.

3.3 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors use the website and Service. In the EU/EEA and UK, we request consent before using non-essential analytics cookies unless an applicable exemption applies.

3.4 Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies may be used to measure campaigns, show relevant ads, or understand conversions. We only use marketing cookies where enabled and legally permitted, including obtaining consent where required.

3.5 Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies may be set by external services such as analytics providers, embedded content providers, payment processors, or customer-support tools.

4. Cookie Consent
